Discover Everest

Introduction

Mount Everest, known as Sagarmatha in Nepal and Chomolungma in Tibet, is the highest mountain in the world, standing at 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet) above sea level. It attracts adventurers from all over the globe who attempt to reach its summit.

Location

Everest is located in the Mahalangur Himal sub-range of the Himalayas, straddling the border between Nepal and the autonomous region of Tibet in China.

Historical Significance

Early Exploration

The mountain was first identified as the world's highest peak in 1856 by the Great Trigonometrical Survey of India. It was named after Sir George Everest, the surveyor general of India from 1830 to 1843.

First Successful Ascent

On May 29, 1953, Sir Edmund Hillary of New Zealand and Tenzing Norgay, a Sherpa of Nepal, became the first climbers confirmed to have reached the summit of Everest.

Interesting Facts about Everest

Mount Everest
Grows approximately 4 mm (0.16 inches) per year due to geological uplift.
The first woman to reach the summit
was Junko Tabei of Japan, on May 16, 1975.
The summit of Everest is covered by snow and ice all year round.
Climbers face extreme weather conditions, with temperatures dropping as low as -60°C (-76°F).
Everest has two main climbing routes
the southeast ridge from Nepal and the north ridge from Tibet.
